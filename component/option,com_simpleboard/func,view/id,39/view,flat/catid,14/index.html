 Re:Interactive City Welcome - 2006/04/27 01:10 It might be that new utopias can still be constructed.

One way of looking at it, as has been suggeted by Morgan and Portella, is that former utopias were often based on nations supposed to be modern at a certain point in history. France for the European monarchies after 1789, "The American Dream" in the Europe of the 50ies, "Skandinavian style and beauty" for postwar Germans, ... With a global deadlock of threats, war and economic crisis, as we experience it now, there is no super-hero nation or country which could serve as a mould for utopian dreams. An utopia would probably to be cross-national, cross-cultural and thereby much harder to identify.
